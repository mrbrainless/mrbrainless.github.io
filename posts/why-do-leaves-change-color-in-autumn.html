Why Do Leaves Change Color in Autumn?

Autumn brings a beautiful transformation in nature — green leaves turn shades of yellow, orange, and red. But what's behind this seasonal color shift? The secret lies in chemistry and the changing light of the seasons.

The Role of Chlorophyll

Leaves are green because of chlorophyll, a pigment that helps plants absorb sunlight for photosynthesis. It’s constantly produced during the growing season to keep the plant energized.

Shorter Days, Less Chlorophyll

As days get shorter and temperatures drop, trees prepare for winter. They stop producing chlorophyll, and the green color fades away. This reveals other pigments that were always there but hidden.

The Hidden Colors Appear

  • Carotenoids – yellow and orange pigments (also found in carrots)
  • Anthocyanins – red and purple pigments produced in autumn
  • Tannins – brown pigments that dominate in some tree types

Why Different Trees Have Different Colors

Tree species and weather conditions affect the intensity and mix of colors. For example, maple trees are known for bright reds due to high anthocyanin levels.

Fun Fact: Warm sunny days followed by cool (but not freezing) nights produce the brightest fall colors!

Summary Table

Pigment Color Source

What Happens After the Color Show?

Eventually, leaves fall off as the tree seals off the base of each leaf. This helps conserve energy and water for winter survival.
